## i
symbols: diary, the pen, the nib, and the ink, big brother, telescreen, ministry of truth building, the young, sexless girl

thoughtcrime: what is illegal under the Party. e.g, Winston's diary, the act of writing down his personal thoughts. 

brainwashing - although Winston is deeply unsettled with the Party's oppression, he's only capable of writing the date at first. he quotes, 

> "He dipped the pen in the ink and faltered for just a second. (...) In small clumsy letters, he wrote: April 4th, 1984. (...) It was curious that he seemed not merely to have lost the power of expressing himself, but even to have forgotten what it was that he had originally intended to say. For weeks past he had been making ready for this moment, and it had never crossed his mind that anything would be needed except courage. The actual writing would be easy. All he had to do was transfer to paper the interminable restless monologue that had been running in his head, literally for years. At this moment, however, even the monologue had dried up." 

> (Orwell 7).

this passage captures his first attempt to write in his diary. it's evident in his hesitation that although he possesses a deep unsettlement with the Party, his cognitive ability to think critically for himself has already been deeply scarred.

at surface level, winston falters as he begins the actions of writing, which signals anxiety and fear. although the act is physically simple, it becomes psychologically difficult. a small detail; his letters are small and clumsy. this suggests that writing is not commonly used under the Party. on a deeper analysis, we realize that his inability to write natively implies a manifestation of internal breakdown via the Party's subjugation.

winston reveals that he perceives himself to possess an "interminable restless monologue". this detail hints that while he did have continuous narration and resistance inside his mind, he collapses the moment he tries to internalize it. this may present to be a key source of tension throughout the novel; that private thought exists in "the few square inches inside one's skull", yet under a totalitarian regime, this personal resistance becomes fragile and impossible to manifest.
