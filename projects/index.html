MD note

MDnote is a vanilla text editor. it doesn't save to any cloud, and all notes are saved to your browser's IndexedDB. notes can be exported as .JSON files and imported into editors. notes are also able to be exported as .txt or .pdf documents. exporting from a root page also exports all children of the root whereas branches, it's only the singular branch page. personally i'm probably most proud of the treemap feature. you can press ctrl t or click the sidebar to view a comprehensive list of your notes hierarchically, and you can also infinitely nest branch pages inside root pages. there are a bunch of hotkeys also available via the hotkey page. you can collapse the sidebar. you can also view a mini treemap with expandable/collapsible trees in the sidebar as well. there's also light mode support if you're one of them. weirdos lol. personally i really like the website and you should probably give it a try. well, you could probably also say why not just use notion or obsidian? i admit obsidian is amazing resource. i really envy their web thing. i also do use it a lot. same with notion, but that's mostly for calendar management. the reason i still built mdnote is because i can't use obsidian on the district laptops, and notion is a huge, bloated app that takes a full two minutes to load. meanwhile, mdnote is completely FOSS (free and open source) and also extremely lightweight (only a few tens of KB i think). it loads instantly. plus though you can also nest in notion you can't really see everything in an entire tree structure. anyways, give it a try. markdown isn't fully supported yet and i don't have immediate plans to fix that. currently mdnote supports underlining, italics, and headings. bolding is buggy. and image resizing is currently questionable but the rest of the app works fine. i mostly made it for myself so it might not have some features you would want. maybe reach out. my guestbook is linked in the nav bar. i may or may not implement the requested feature.
